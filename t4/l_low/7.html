Phishing Behavior Training

Lexical behavior

Lexical behavior involves using specific words, terms, or verbs in a message to enhance the sender’s intention to mislead a receiver. This type consists of four specific behaviors.

Foreshadowing words
Foreshadowing word is a list of words hinting at the possibility of phishing such as ‘update, confirm, verify, secure, log, click, inconvenient, account, go, carry, run.’ For instance, in the sentence “Click the button below to update your personal information”, the word "click" is used.

Generalizing terms
This behavior involves using generalizing terms such as everyone, all, anybody. For example, in the sentence, “Everyone believes that our economy will improve next year”, a generalizing term ‘everyone’ is used.

Exclusive words
This behavior involves using exclusive words such as ‘but, except, without.’ For example, in the sentence, “Benefit is clear without loss”, an exclusive word ‘without’ is used.
