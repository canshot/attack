Reading Training Material

  • The receiver may be aware of the behaviors that you will learn and when he/she makes detection decisions of phishing emails.

  • To be successful in a phishing attack, you should try your best to RESTRICT yourself from displaying those behaviors while staying on the topic.


Structural behavior

The structural behavior concerns manipulating the structural elements of the emails, such as email address and sent time. This behavior consists of five specific behaviors.

Link-embedding words
You can think of the words with a link including ‘click here’, ‘log in’, or ‘update’. When email receivers click the link-embedding words, they can go to another web page.

Deceptive link
Deceptive link is the technique in which the phisher includes the full address of a fake link in the email. You can insert a link that contains a suspicious address like the following example. “Refer to the NYT article to read their corruption on www.nytime.net/article/12543.”

Multiple body parts
This behavior involves multiple body parts more than one paragraph in the email body.

Unusual sent time
This behavior involves the sent time manipulation. For instance, you may use an unusual sent time, such as 3:35 am.

Email address with a suspicious domain
The suspicious domain is the falsification technique in which the phisher includes an unverified domain in the sender’s email address. For example, by using a fake email address, such as insurance@car.com, or bestheadhunter@linkedin.net, email senders can allude to that he or she holds domain expertise, which attempts to hoax email receivers.
