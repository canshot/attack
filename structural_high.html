Structural behavior



The structural behavior concerns manipulating the structural elements of the emails, such as email address and sent time. This behavior consists of five specific behaviors.

1.
The first behavior involves using link-embedding words. You can think of the words with a link including ‘click here’, ‘log in’, or ‘update’. When email receivers click the link-embedding words, they can go to another web page.

2.
The second link behavior is using specious link. You can insert a link that contains a fake URL address as the following example. “Refer to the NYT article to read their corruption (www.nytime.net/article/12543).”

3.
The first structural behavior involves using a fake group email address. For example, All_student_announcement@uncc.edu is a fake group email address.

4.
The second structural behavior involves sent time manipulation. For instance, you may notice the sent time as an unusual one. A business email sent at 03:35 AM is very uncommon.

5.
The third structural behavior is using email address falsification to make a spurious impression to the receivers. For example, by using a fake email address, such as insurance@car.com, or bestheadhunter@linkedin.net, email senders can allude to that he or she holds domain expertise, which attempts to hoax email receivers.
