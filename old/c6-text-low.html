Lexical behavior

3.
The third lexical behavior involves using generalizing terms.

Structural behavior



The structural behavior concerns manipulating the structural elements of the emails, such as email address and sent time. This behavior consists of five specific behaviors.

1.
The first behavior involves link-embedding words.

2.
The second behavior is using a specious link.

3.
The third structural behavior involves using a fake group email address.

4.
The fourth structural behavior involves the sent time manipulation.

5.
The last structural behavior is using email address falsification to make a spurious impression to the receivers.

Discourse behavior



The discourse can be defined as a sequence of sentences that have internal relations to themselves as well as external to other sentences. The discourse behavior concerns linking part of a message in online interaction to the entire message as a whole that looks beyond individual sentences. There are two specific behaviors.

1.
The first discourse behavior is using mismatch subjects. That is, the subject line of an email is irrelevant or does not match the content in the body of the email.

2.
The second discourse behavior is using disguised sentences. With this behavior, an email sender disguises the subject line and the content of an email as a reply to the email receiver.
