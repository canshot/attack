Reading Training Material

  • The receiver may be aware of the behaviors that you will learn and when he/she makes detection decisions of phishing emails.

  • Therefore, to be successful in a phishing attack, you should try your best to RESTRICT yourself from displaying those behaviors while staying on the topic.


Structural behavior

The structural behavior concerns manipulating the structural elements of the emails, such as email address and sent time. This behavior consists of five specific behaviors.

Link-embedding words
You can think of the words with a link including ‘click here’, ‘log in’, or ‘update’. When email receivers click the link-embedding words, they can go to another web page.

Specious link
You can insert a link that contains a fake URL address as the following example. “Refer to the NYT article to read their corruption (www.nytime.net/article/12543).”

Fake group email address
This behavior involves using a fake group email address.

For example, All_student_announcement@uncc.edu is a fake group email address.

Sent time manipulation
This behavior involves the sent time manipulation.

For instance, you may use an usual sent time, such as 3:35 am.

Email address falsification
This behavior is using email address falsification to make a spurious impression to the receivers.

For example, by using a fake email address, such as insurance@car.com, or bestheadhunter@linkedin.net, email senders can allude to that he or she holds domain expertise, which attempts to hoax email receivers.
