3. Instuctions to write a phishing email

  • The phishing email should contain your intention to deceive by misleading a receiver into disclosing his/her personal information (e.g., password and credit card number)
  • The email should communicate information about the assigned topic/event with more than 50 words and exploit the receivers’ personal information (e.g., name, academic major) that is provided to you.
  • Add 'a call to action sentence' in the email which leads the receiver to click the link.
  • You may NOT directly copy/paste the emails found on the Internet.
  • A successful phishing attack is indicated by successfully deceiving the email receiver without being suspected as a phishing email.
